Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

1941-2011 An american computer pioneer

About

Dennis Ritchie was born in Bronxsville, New York. His father was Alistair E. Ritchie, a longtime Bell Labs scientist. Dennis graduated from Harvard University with degrees in physics and applied mathematics. He created the C programming language and, along with colleague Ken Thompson, the UNIX operating system. He worked at Bell Labs most of his life. The same company his father had dedicated his career to. Bell Labs is and was arguably the leading research organization in Information Technology and Communications.

    Awards

  • 1982- IEEE Emanuel R. Piore Award
  • 1983- Turing Award
  • 1990- IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal
  • 1999- National Medal of Technology and Innovation
  • 2005- Industrial Research Institute Achievement Award
  • 2011- Japan Prize
